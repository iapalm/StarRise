Spectral classification

Spectral classification is simply the categorization of stars based on the light emitted from the star. The Morgan-Keenan system (MKK) is the most widespread nomenclature system today. This system uses the letters O, B, A, F, G, K, M, L, T, and Y to denote the temperature from coolest to warmest, respectively. Each class is subdivided into 10 categories, with 0 being the coolest and 9 being the warmest, so B6 < B9 < A0 in temperature. The MKK system also measures luminosity using Roman numerals. The measure of luminosity is based off of the width of the spectral lines of the star. O denotes hypergiants, I denotes supergiants, II denotes bright giants, class III denotes normal giants, class IV denotes sub-giants, class V denotes main sequence stars, class VI denotes sub-dwarfs, VII denotes white dwarfs, and VIII denotes brown dwarfs, the dimmest of the stars.
