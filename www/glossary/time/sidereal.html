Sidereal time:

 

Sidereal time is a time scale based on the Earth's rotation relative to the stars. One sidereal day is equivalent to the time elapsed between two consecutive points in time where a star is on an observer's local meridian. A sidereal day is 99.726958% of the length of a solar day, or 3 minutes and 56 seconds shorter. A star's right ascension and declination are used in conjunction with local sidereal time to determine a star's position in the sky.

The local sidereal time for any given location in any point in time will gain 4 minutes for every 24 hours of local time until one year has passed and one additional sidereal day has passed.
